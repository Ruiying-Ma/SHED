Higher production  costs  are  experienced  at  the  beginning  of  a  new  or  derivative  aircraft  program.  Units  produced  early  in  a  program  require substantially more effort (labor and other resources) than units produced later in a program because of volume efficiencies and the effects of learning.   We  expect  that  these  deferred  costs  will  be  fully  recovered  when  all  units  included  in  the  accounting  quantity  are  delivered  as  the expected unit cost for later deliveries is below the estimated average cost of all units in the program. Supplier advances represent payments for parts we have contracted to receive from suppliers in the future. As parts are received, supplier advances are amortized to work in process.
