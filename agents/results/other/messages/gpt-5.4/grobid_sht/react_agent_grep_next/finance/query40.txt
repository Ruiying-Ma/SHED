1.104 | Cash Flows from Operating Activities
Our cash flows from operating activities primarily include net income adjusted for (i) non-cash items included in net income, such as provisions for credit losses, depreciation and amortization, stock-based compensation, deferred taxes and other non-cash items and (ii) changes in the balances of operating assets and liabilities, which can vary significantly in the normal course of business due to the amount and timing of payments. Operating margin is not a particularly useful metric for American Express because it is a financial services/payments company whose results are presented around total revenues net of interest expense, provisions for credit losses, and pretax income rather than operating income. Based on the reported figures, pretax income as a share of total revenues net of interest expense declined from about 25.2% in 2021 ($10,689 million on $42,380 million) to about 18.1% in 2022 ($9,585 million on $52,862 million), so its margin profile did not improve in 2022.
