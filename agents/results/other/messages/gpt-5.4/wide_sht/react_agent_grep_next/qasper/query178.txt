3.1 Models and Datasets
We choose BERT ( Devlin et al. , 2019 ) and RoBERTa ( Liu et al. , 2019 ) as the subjects of our study, since they represent state of the art and the same architecture. XLNet ( Yang et al. , 2019 ) is another alternative; however, they use a slightly different attention structure, and our preliminary experiments encountered difficulties in reproducibility with the Transformers library.  Each model has base and large variants that con- tain 12 and 24 layers, respectively. We denote them by appending the variant name as a subscript to the model name.Within each variant, the two models display slight variability in parameter count—110 and 125 million in the base variant, and 335 and 355 in the large one. These differences are mostly at- tributed to RoBERTa using many more embedding parameters—exactly 63% more for both variants.
