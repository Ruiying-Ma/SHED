3M’s operating income margin declined to 19.1% in 2022 from 20.8% in 2021 on a GAAP basis, while adjusted operating margin declined to 20.8% from 22.2%; the company says the change was driven by a 2.4-point negative raw material/logistics impact, a 0.3-point decline from lower disposable respirator demand, foreign currency pressure, divestiture impacts, and higher pension/postretirement non-service expense, partly offset by a 1.0-point benefit from organic growth/productivity and other factors including strong pricing, spending discipline, and restructuring actions. GAAP margin was also heavily affected by special items, including significant litigation costs, divestiture costs, Russia exit charges, and PFAS manufacturing exit costs, partly offset by a gain on the Food Safety divestiture.
