Abstract
Natural language inference (NLI) aims at pre- dicting the relationship between a given pair of premise and hypothesis. However, several works have found that there widely exists a bias pattern called annotation artifacts in NLI datasets, making it possible to identify the la- bel only by looking at the hypothesis. This irregularity makes the evaluation results over- estimated and affects models’ generalization ability. In this paper, we consider a more trust- worthy setting, i.e.  , cross-dataset evaluation.
