Our Spend-Centric Model and Revenue Mix
Our “spend-centric” business model focuses on generating revenues primarily by driving spending on our cards and secondarily through finance charges and fees. Spending on our cards, which is higher on average on a per-card basis versus our competitors, offers superior value to merchants in the form of loyal customers and larger transactions.  Because of the revenues generated from having high-spending Card Members and the annual card fees we charge on many of our products, we are able to invest in attractive rewards and other benefits for Card Members, as well as targeted marketing and other programs and investments for merchants. This creates incentives for Card Members to spend more on their cards and positively differentiates American Express cards.We believe our spend-centric model gives us the ability to provide differentiated value to Card Members, merchants and business partners.

================================== Ai Message ==================================

Gross margin is not a useful metric for American Express because this report presents results as total revenues net of interest expense, provisions for credit losses, and operating expenses such as Card Member rewards, business development, Card Member services, marketing, salaries and employee benefits, and other expenses, rather than cost of goods sold and gross profit. For FY2022, the main operating changes were that total revenues net of interest expense increased primarily due to higher billed business, growth in premium card portfolios, higher foreign exchange-related revenues, travel commissions and fees, delinquency fees, higher processed volumes, and higher average Card Member loan balances and interest rates, while expenses increased due to higher Card Member rewards, business development, Card Member services, marketing, salaries and employee benefits, and other expenses.
